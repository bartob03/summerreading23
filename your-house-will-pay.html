Late summer reading 2023

Your House Will Pay (2020)

The 1990s riots in Los Angeles was an unpleasant time in history, and Steph Cha's crime thriller, "Your House Will Pay" pulls no punches in depicting the many racial, socio-economic, and political issues of that time and its continuing effects today. The story revolves around Shawn Mathews, an African-American ex-convict, and Grace Park, a Korean-American immigrant, as they come to grips with generational racial tensions after the death of Mathews' sister and after Grace's mother survives a shooting. Shawn's family is haunted by horrible memories of his sister's death and with the release of Ray from prison, Shawn is wondering if history may repeat itself. Meanwhile, Grace's attempts to reconcile her sister and mother are stymied by elements from the family's past, which continues to be a dagger over their heads in the present.

Steph Cha strengthens the familial conflict by using minute details from police procedures, media coverage practices, and real-live events. While "Your House Will Pay" is fictional, it is deeply anchored in the reality of racism, grief, anger, and violence. In Cha's novel, Los Angeles becomes a stage for tragedy and trauma.
