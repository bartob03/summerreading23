Late summer reading 2023

Starman: Bowie’s Stardust Years (2023)

Even if you aren't familiar with David Bowie's music, Reinhard Kleist's "Starman: Bowie's Stardust Years," originally published in German and available in English, gives you an insight as to how influential Bowie's music, career, and persona were to European, British, and American culture. In "Starman", Bowie never flaunts his gender fluidity, but is depicted as a musician unashamed of his authentic persona and bombastic presence.  Kleist illustrates Bowie's seminal years with verve and enthusiasm, his colors reflecting Bowie's evolution and growth as an artist and celebrity. "Starman" isn't an illustrated biography of the revered musician, but rather a homage to his global image and accomplishments. Kleist makes use of various sources such as interviews, news articles, and performances to chart the chaotic rise of David Bowie. His panels and color palette pay respect to 50s and 60s UK comic books, while never deviating from his "Stardust years" (a follow up graphic novel will take up his "Berlin years").   The 1960s was a lifetime ago and there is no doubt that today's generation of readers may not quite appreciate Kleist's rough and unfamiliar depiction of the period. The occasionally disturbing rock n'roll lifestyle of the time may elicit mixed emotions, but no one who reads "Starman: Bowie's Stardust Years" can deny that a graphic novel is a good medium for an authentic depiction of a real and explosive legend such as David Bowie.
