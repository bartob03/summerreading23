Late summer reading 2023

Ring Shout (2020)

Ring Shout (2020) is a mix of horror, adventure, and science fiction, with monsters in both human (Klans) and inhuman (Ku Kluxes) forms. Clark uses real historical artefacts such as Jim Crow segregation, Georgia, and African-American foklore, to anchor his brisk story about three Black women fighting against evil. While Clark tries to incorporate various elements in this sub-200 page story, it never really bogs down Cordelia, Maryse, and Sadie's uphill and terrifying adventure. The short length allows readers to choose to ignore the social commentary that can be read between the lines, and focus instead in the chilling and rousing events. However, Clark's enthusiasm in his theme can be felt in his characters. Moreover, the evil component never feels superficial, weak, and ephemeral, especially when compared with more popular media from American (e.g. Marvel and DC) and Japan (e.g. manga). The hate from bigots and racists in the story is suffocating and overwhelming, more so than the supernatural creatures that assault the heroic trio. Ring Shout is one of those books that can be finished in one sitting, and yet remain with you for an extra hour or two.
