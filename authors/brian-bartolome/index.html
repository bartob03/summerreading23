Ring Shout (2020)

"Ring Shout" (2020) is a mix of horror, adventure, and science fiction, with monsters in both human (Klans) and inhuman (Ku Kluxes) forms. Clark uses real historical artefacts such as Jim Crow segregation, Georgia, and African American folklore, to anchor his brisk story about three Black women fighting against evil.

I Have Loved Me a Man: The Life and Times of Mika (2018)

"I Have Loved Me a Man: The Life and Times of Mika" (2018) is equal parts history, celebrity biography, and critical race theory. Mazer's book deals with Mika, a queer Maori theatre performer who witnesses, among many events in New Zealand, ethnic cleansing and the rise of the indigenous performer.
