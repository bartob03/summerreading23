Late summer reading 2023

Mexican Gothic (2020)

"Mexican Gothic" by Silvia Moreno-Garcia starts simply enough as impulsive protagonist Noemi Taboada finds herself visiting her ailing newlywed cousin in a Mexican pueblo at the request of her father. With every intention of comforting her cousin, Noemi stays at the High Palace longer than needed and begins to experience disturbing dreams. The behavior of her cousin's new husband, the daunting aunt, the patriarch of the home, and the youngest child at High Palace, seems to signal danger not only to her cousin, but to herself as well. It is obvious to Noemi, however, that secrets abound at High Palace, though she is ultimately unsure if it is her duty to uncover them. 

Moreno-Garcia builds the horrific atmosphere slowly, somewhat dispensing with typical Gothic tropes while ensuring the reader is aware of them at the same time. As Noemi finds herself taking on more than what she expected, the reader realizes the novel is more than just about the travails of a plucky 21-year old, but also about abuse, misogynism, and racism. Although the novel has a modern 1950s setting, Noemi's cushy background and the more depressed circumstances of her cousin's new family calls back classic horror stories of high-born/posh protagonists trapped in an unfriendly and unfamiliar medieval locale.
