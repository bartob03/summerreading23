I Have Loved Me a Man: The Life and Times of Mika (2018)

"I Have Loved Me a Man: The Life and Times of Mika" (2018) is equal parts history, celebrity biography, and critical race theory. Mazer's book deals with Mika, a queer Maori theatre performer who witnesses, among many events in New Zealand, ethnic cleansing and the rise of the indigenous performer. Mazer narrates small yet significant events and people that influenced Mika, from lesser-known talents to filmmakers to global celebrities such as Grace Jones. The book is filled with colorful photos that chart his film, theatre, and live performances. Mika's homosexuality is dealt with evenly and links all the important events of his life to his gayness, while never ignoring his cultural roots. "I have Loved Me a Man" also tracks his developments as an artist in parallel, even as it details the many deaths of Mika's friends and lovers throughout his eventful career.
