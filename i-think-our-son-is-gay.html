I Think Our Son is Gay / Uchi no Musuko wa Tabun Gay (2020)

I Think Our Son is Gay / Uchi no Musuko wa Tabun Gay (2020) is a charming, slice of life manga from Okura. The story revolves around a boy who does not quite understand he is gay and is unsure about how he should respond to this reality. As he navigates simple day-to-day events, this confusion leads him to quite a few humorous situations. Unlike most Western novels, and even American comic books, Uchi no Musuko wa Tabun Gay does not make a big deal about homosexuality at all.  The five volume series is light-hearted and there is no real conflict in the series. His budding homosexuality is a characteristic rather than a problem or issue. High school student Hiroki is familiar to anyone who has spent some time in a typical high school environment. Those not familiar with the Asian/Japanese setting, or manga in general, might have problems relating to the relationships within the Aoyama home or at his public school. Nevertheless, the laugh-out-loud dialogue and the semi-awkward events that occur between Hiroki, his brother Yuri, his loving mom Tomoko, and other supporting characters make the books a pleasant read. Unlike other manga and anime characters, Hiroki is a truly likable character as he deals with his attraction to boys - there is no exaggerated caricatures or superfluous features to Hiroki as often seen in Japanese media.

Okura's art and script is wonderfully restrained with the facial expressions and reactions of his characters familiar, clear, and warm.  The volumes are a rare treat that deserve a second or even third or fourth reading.
